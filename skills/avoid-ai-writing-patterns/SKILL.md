## Word choice

Avoid words that LLMs overuse: additionally (sentence-initial), align with, bolster, boasts, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, encompass, foster, cultivate, garner, groundbreaking, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate / intricacies, key (adjective), landscape (abstract noun), meticulous / meticulously, pivotal, profound, resonate, showcase, tapestry (abstract noun), testament, underscore (verb), vibrant.

Also avoid the set phrases: stands as, serves as, marks a shift, setting the stage for, evolving landscape, focal point, indelible mark, deeply rooted, diverse array, natural beauty, nestled, in the heart of, commitment to, rich cultural heritage.

## Grammar

Use "is" and "are" freely. Prefer "the library has four modules" over "the library boasts four modules" or "the library features four modules". Prefer "the committee was formed in 1989" over "the committee was established, marking a pivotal moment in the evolution of regional governance". Prefer "she was a candidate" over "she ventured into politics as a candidate".

Do not frame statements as correcting a misconception. Avoid "not just X, but also Y" and "not X, but Y". State the fact directly.

Do not repeat a keyword with synonyms to avoid using the same word twice. Reuse the right word. Substitute only when the new word carries a different meaning.

## Content

Say what the thing is. Do not attach analysis of its significance, legacy, or broader implications to factual statements. Do not end sentences with "-ing" phrases that editorialize: "highlighting its enduring importance", "reflecting broader trends", "contributing to the evolving landscape", "symbolizing its ongoing relevance".

Do not attribute claims to unnamed authorities. "Experts argue", "observers have noted", "industry reports show", and "several sources indicate" are not citations. Name the source or drop the claim. Do not present a single source as evidence of wide consensus.

Do not inflate the subject's significance. Do not add statements about how something "represents a shift", "shaped the region", or "plays a vital role in the ecosystem" when the source material does not say so. Do not add conservation status paragraphs, future-prospects speculation, or heritage-importance commentary unless directly requested.

Do not write a "challenges and future prospects" closing. Do not end with "despite these challenges, X continues to thrive" or speculation about what ongoing initiatives might achieve.

## Tone

Write the way a factual report would, not the way a press release or travel brochure would. Do not describe places as nestled, scenic, or vibrant. Do not describe organizations as committed to excellence. Do not describe products as boasting features or showcasing craftsmanship.

## Format

Do not use numbered lists. Plain prose serves most purposes better. Unnumbered lists are acceptable when items have no natural prose order and listing genuinely aids reading rather than substituting for prose.

Do not use em dashes as a stylistic device to punch up clauses. Use a comma, parentheses, or a colon instead.

Do not add a conclusion paragraph that restates what was already said. End when the content is done.
